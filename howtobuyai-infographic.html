How to buy AI

A buyer's framework for making smarter decisions

The three golden rules of AI buying

1

Define your problem in dollars

Align your evaluation to a specific, measurable business problem, not a vague symptom. Quantify the cost to anchor your ROI calculation.

2

Align the price to your outcome

Ensure the vendor's pricing metric scales directly with the value you receive. You should only pay more when your business demonstrably wins more.

3

Demand guardrails to control costs

Implement contractual safeguards like usage caps and alerts. This provides budget predictability and prevents bill shock.

What business problem are you trying to solve?

Improving Team Productivity

e.g., better collaboration, faster content creation, augmenting individual skills

AI Value: Enabling individual users. Value grows as more people use it.

Expect: A Per-User/Seat Model.

Key Question: "Is the per-seat cost less than the productivity gain per person?"

Automating a High-Volume Process

e.g., processing documents, analyzing data feeds, handling routine tasks

AI Value: Processing volume or throughput. Value grows with more operations completed.

Expect: A Usage/Consumption Model.

Key Question: "What is the unit cost per operation, and how does it decrease at scale?"

Achieving a Specific Business Result

e.g., reducing customer churn, increasing sales conversions, resolving support tickets

AI Value: Delivering specific, measurable outcomes. Value is tied to results.

Expect: An Outcome-Based Model.

Key Question: "How do you define a successful outcome, and how do we audit it?"

Vendor scorecard checklist

✓

Aligned with Value: Pricing maps to the business outcome *we* care about.

✓

Predictable: Vendor provides tools or a model to forecast our costs.

✓

Acceptable: The model is simple and intuitive for all stakeholders to understand.

✓

Scalable: The unit price decreases as our usage grows 10x or 100x.

✓

Consumable: The model fits how our organization budgets for and purchases technology.

✓

Auditable: Billable metrics can be clearly measured and verified to avoid disputes.
