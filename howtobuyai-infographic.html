How to buy AI

An interactive buyer's framework for making smarter decisions

The three golden rules of AI buying

1

Define your problem in dollars

The "Why": Don't start with the AI; start with the P&L. Anchor your evaluation to a specific, measurable business problem, not a vague symptom like "improving efficiency." This gives you a powerful basis for negotiating price.

How to Do It: Find a known, quantified pain. Ask diagnostic questions like, "What specific manual process are we trying to automate?" or "If this AI works perfectly, what specific cost disappears from the P&L?"

2

Align the price to your outcome

The "Why": A fair model is one where the vendor only makes more money when your business gets more value. You should only pay more when your business demonstrably wins more.

Red Flag: Watch for pricing that charges for "activity" instead of "outcomes" (e.g., paying per API call regardless of success). This incentivizes vendor activity, not your results.

3

Demand guardrails to control costs

The "Why": Pure usage-based models without protection expose you to "bill shock." A few power users or an unexpected spike in demand can blow up your entire budget. You must de-risk the financial commitment.

What to Demand: Make these non-negotiable in your contract: hard spending caps (monthly/quarterly), automated usage alerts (e.g., at 70% and 90% of quota), and the option for pre-paid credits.

Vendor scorecard checklist

✓Aligned with Value

Your Key Question: "Does the pricing metric map directly to the business outcome *we* care about?"

✓Predictable

Your Key Question: "What tools do you provide to help us forecast and control costs, avoiding bill shock?"

✓Scalable

Your Key Question: "As our usage grows 10x, how does the per-unit cost decrease? Show us the pricing tiers."

✓Auditable

Your Key Question: "How can we independently measure and verify the billable metrics to avoid disputes?"

✓Acceptable

Your Key Question: "Is the model simple enough for all stakeholders (Finance, Legal) to understand?"

✓Consumable

Your Key Question: "Does this model fit how our organization budgets for and purchases technology?"

✓Sustainable

Your Key Question: "Is pricing protected from underlying AI model cost volatility?"

✓Differentiable

Your Key Question: "How is your pricing model clearly better aligned with our success than competitors' models?"
